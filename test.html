Pancreatic Cancer: Symptoms and Signs

You will find more information about changes in the body and other things that may indicate a problem that requires medical attention. To see other pages, use the menu.

People with pancreatic cancer may experience the following symptoms or signs. Sometimes people with pancreatic cancer do not have any of these changes. Or the cause of these symptoms may be a different medical condition other than cancer.

Doctors often say that pancreatic cancer is a "silent disease" because it does not have many noticeable symptoms early on. Also, at present, there are no tests that can reliably detect cancer in people who do not have symptoms. When people have symptoms, they are similar to symptoms of other medical conditions, such as ulcers or pancreatitis (see the Risk Factors section  ). As the cancer grows, symptoms can include:

  • Yellowing of the skin and eyes, darkening of the urine, itching, and clay-colored stools, which are signs of jaundice caused by blocked bile ducts

  • Pain in the upper abdomen or back

  • Painful swelling of an arm or leg due to a blood clot

  • Burning sensation in the stomach or other gastrointestinal discomfort

  • Stomach bloating

  • Floating stools with a particularly foul odor and unusual color, due to the body not digesting fats well

  • Soft spot

  • Loss of appetite

  • Nausea and vomiting

  • Shaking chills

  • Fever

  • Weight loss for no apparent reason

If you are concerned about any changes you have experienced, speak with your doctor. Your doctor will ask you how long and how often you have experienced the symptom (s), among other questions. This is to help find the cause of the problem, called a diagnosis .

If cancer is diagnosed, symptom relief remains an important aspect of cancer care and treatment. This may be called hospice or supportive care. Be sure to talk to your healthcare team about the symptoms you are experiencing, including any new symptoms or changes in symptoms.
